RIVERSIDE UNIVERSITY — ADMISSIONS POLICY DOCUMENT

APPLICATION DEADLINES
- Fall semester: May 1
- Spring semester: November 1
- Late applications accepted within 14-day grace period with dean approval

MISSING DOCUMENTS
- Students have 14 days from application date to submit missing documents
- Missing items: transcripts, recommendation letters, test scores, financial aid forms
- After 14 days, application is placed on hold and student is notified

FINANCIAL AID ELIGIBILITY
- Need-based grant: EFC below $5,000 qualifies
- EFC $0–$2,000: up to $5,000 award
- EFC $2,001–$3,500: up to $4,000 award
- EFC $3,501–$5,000: up to $2,500 award
- Merit scholarships: GPA 3.8+ qualifies for $2,000 annual award

DEFERRAL REQUESTS
- Must be submitted at least 30 days before term start date
- Maximum deferral period: one academic year
- Student must reconfirm enrollment 60 days before deferred term

EMAIL TONE GUIDELINES
- Professional but warm
- Address student by first name
- Include specific deadlines and next steps
- Avoid jargon

ESCALATION TRIGGERS
- Financial aid appeals over $5,000
- Medical or personal hardship cases
- Any situation requiring policy exception
- Legal or compliance questions
